Tradeoffs at a glance

Dimension A. Sky + Signal Green B. Deep Azure + Emerald C. Cobalt + Lime
Product continuity Highest. Direct match to Planned/Spare bars. Website and product feel identical. High. Close family of the sampled colors, just deeper. Users still recognize the palette. Medium. Same hue families but noticeably more saturated — marketing-forward.
Personality Friendly, approachable, slightly soft. Reads as "utility product." Confident, modern, data-forward. Serious without being corporate. Energetic, bold, slightly startup-y. Strong visual hit.
CTA weight Weak. Sky blue CTAs feel like disabled buttons next to competitors' deep colors. Strong. #1e6fbf carries real CTA weight without going corporate navy. Very strong. Cobalt is the heaviest CTA color of the three.
Contrast on white Both fail AA for body text. Accent-only usage required. Both pass AA. Usable for text, icons, links. Blue passes AA easily; lime fails on white (accent/icon only).
Dark mode Washes out. Sky blue + soft green both lose saturation against dark bg. Best of the three. Emerald on dark navy is canonical "intelligent product" aesthetic. Lime pops but reads gamer/crypto on dark. Risk of wrong category signal.
AI module framing Weak — feels like any utility dashboard. Strong. Emerald reads as "signal / healthy / intelligent" across SaaS (Supabase, Linear, Vercel). Mixed. Lime is attention-grabbing but not category-appropriate for data/AI.
Print/PDF reports Risk of looking washed out in printed Power BI reports at small sizes. Excellent. Both colors hold shape and saturation in PDF/print exports. Cobalt prints well; lime turns muddy in most print conditions.

Why B is the strong default

Three things about your context push toward Deep Azure + Emerald:

  • You keep the blue/green identity users already associate with Proxuma — just at a depth that can actually carry headline CTAs and pass contrast on white. Option A is too pale to anchor a marketing page; users would scan right past the buy buttons.
  • Emerald is the AI-era green. Supabase, Linear dark, Vercel analytics, Cursor — the whole "intelligent developer tool" category uses emerald as its signal accent. For an AI copilot product launching dark mode, this green pre-sells the positioning.
  • It survives the dark-mode transition you're planning. Option A fades to near-invisible on dark; emerald on navy is peak modern SaaS.
Use the in-product colors as data colors, not brand colors Your current sky #5B9BD5 and signal green #70BF6F should remain the exact tones inside Planned/Spare bars, chart series, and data viz. That's where brand memory actually lives for users. The marketing site uses the deeper versions (Option B) for CTAs, headline accents, and glow — while the data viz keeps the original friendlier tones. Two layers, same family.
Bottom line

Deep Azure #1e6fbf + Emerald #10b981, with in-product tones preserved for data viz

Use #1e6fbf for CTAs, headline emphasis, and primary nav states. Use #10b981 for accents, success signals, AI module highlights, and dark-mode glow. Keep Proxuma sky #5B9BD5 and signal green #70BF6F reserved as data-viz tones inside dashboards, charts, and Planned/Spare bars. That gives you one palette family at two depths: marketing uses the deep version for weight, the product uses the original tones for continuity.

Dials if you want to tune

  • Want a softer anchor? Swap deep azure to #2b7bcf — a half-step back toward the original sky.
  • Want a richer accent? Swap emerald to #059669 (deeper) for more gravitas, or #22c55e (brighter) for more energy.
  • Want a warm neutral as a fourth tone? Add #f5efe4 (sand) or #faf6ee (cream) to break up blue+green fatigue on long marketing pages.
