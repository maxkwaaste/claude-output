Keyword Match Type Analysis

The quality scores data shows a major issue: the removed campaigns (now inactive) have dozens of BROAD match keywords like "Autotask Kanban", "Autotask planner", "Autotask Service Call" that were pulling in generic Autotask how-to searches.

Broad Match Risk The "Projects & Planning" ad group (removed campaign) had BROAD match keywords like "best expense management software", "real estate portfolio management software usa", "construction project management software". These have zero relevance to Proxuma's Autotask add-ons. If these campaigns are re-enabled, all broad match keywords must be converted to phrase or exact match first.

The currently active campaigns use mostly PHRASE (P) and ADDED keywords, which is better. But the broad net on generic terms like "msp software" and "msp project management" still catches too many irrelevant searches.

Top 5 Immediate Actions

  1. Set up conversion tracking. Define conversion actions in Google Ads: form submissions, demo requests, trial signups. Without this, every optimization decision is a guess. Do this before spending another euro.
  2. Pause "Targeted campaign autotask" today. It is spending €8.83/click on people looking for free Autotask plugin downloads. Zero commercial intent for Proxuma's products.
  3. Add the 40+ negative keywords listed above. Apply to all campaigns at account level. This stops the worst search term bleed immediately.
  4. Do not re-enable USA/AUS campaigns until conversion tracking is live and negative keywords are in place. Right now they would burn budget on the same irrelevant terms.
  5. Add mobile bid adjustment -50% on non-brand campaigns. B2B MSP buyers do not convert on phones. Desktop CTR is 4x higher than mobile for the brand campaign, and the one mobile click on the Autotask campaign cost €11.

Summary

This account is spending €125/month with nothing to show for it. The root cause is structural: no conversion tracking means no optimization data, and broad/phrase match keywords without proper negatives attract the wrong searches.

The good news: the Brand campaign works fine, and some search terms in the USA campaign show real buying intent for Autotask add-ons. The path forward is simple: fix tracking, add negatives, pause the Autotask campaign, then carefully re-enable USA leads with tighter targeting.

Expected impact of these changes: same brand protection at €9/month, elimination of €26/month Autotask waste, and a cleaner USA campaign that, once re-enabled with conversion tracking, will finally show whether Google Ads can produce leads for Proxuma at a viable cost.
