Google's actual rules (not what you fear)

Google does not penalize content for being AI-generated. They penalize "scaled content abuse" using AI to mass-produce thin pages without user value. An Ahrefs study of 600,000 pages found 86.5% of top-20 ranking pages contain some AI-generated content, with zero correlation between AI usage and ranking position.

What gets punished

  • Fresh domains with 100% raw AI output. A 16-month Search Engine Land experiment showed these pages dropped from 28% in top-100 to 3% after three months.
  • Sites with >80% unedited AI content. Google's March 2025 core update reduced rankings for 61% of these.
  • Zero experience signals. After the March 2026 core update, the "Experience" in E-E-A-T became the primary differentiator. Content lacking it gets demoted regardless of who wrote it.

What ranks well

  • AI-drafted + human-edited content. Performs within 4% of fully human content on median ranking. HubSpot found hybrid content outperformed pure AI by 40% on engagement.
  • First-person experience signals. Specific client scenarios, original data, screenshots of real work, verifiable author credentials.
  • Named author with real expertise. Not "content team." A person with demonstrable background in the topic.
  • Original data that AI cannot fabricate. Case numbers, anonymized client results, proprietary benchmarks.
  • 2,100-2,800 word sweet spot. These are 2.8x more likely to earn AI Overview citations.

The defense strategy

Every article on claudezakelijk.nl is written as your buddy sharing real implementation experience with Claude in Dutch SMBs. Every piece must contain: (1) a specific client scenario (anonymized), (2) the exact Claude prompt used, (3) real output with screenshots, (4) gotchas and what broke, (5) named author with bio and LinkedIn. This is what Google rewards. Not the absence of AI, but the presence of genuine expertise.

Article formula (non-negotiable per piece)

Every article published on claudezakelijk.nl must contain all five elements. No exceptions. This is both the SEO defense and the competitive moat.

1. A real problem solved
E-E-A-T: Experience
Specific client scenario (anonymized). Not "a company wanted to improve their workflow." Instead: "A recruitment firm with 14 consultants spent 6 hours per week manually writing candidate summaries."
2. The exact Claude prompt
E-E-A-T: Expertise
Verbatim prompt, not reconstructed. Shows working knowledge of Claude's capabilities and limits. Readers can copy and test it themselves.
3. Real output with screenshots
E-E-A-T: Evidence
Screenshot or full fragment of what Claude produced. Not paraphrased. This is the signal Google cannot find on competitor content sites that write about Claude without using it.
4. Gotchas and what broke
E-E-A-T: Authenticity
Where Claude failed, what needed manual correction, what the limitations are. This is the hardest part for pure AI content to fake and the strongest ranking signal.
5. Named author with credentials
E-E-A-T: Authority
Full byline with photo, bio, LinkedIn link. "Runs Proxuma (SaaS) and Dxfferent (consulting) using Claude every day." Not "content team."

Content production pipeline

One recording, four outputs. This is the existing plan from the GTM doc, validated against current SEO research.

Input 5-10 min screen recording of real Claude work
  • Record via Loom while solving a real client or internal problem
  • Captures the prompt, the output, the iteration, the gotchas naturally
  • This is the source material AI content farms cannot replicate
Process Claude Code transcribes + generates article skeleton
  • Automated transcription with youtube-transcript-api or Whisper
  • Claude drafts structure following the 5-element formula
  • Human editing pass: 20 minutes to add voice, anecdotes, industry context
Output (4 channels) One recording becomes four assets
  • 1. Kennisbank article on claudezakelijk.nl (targets one long-tail keyword, 2,100-2,800 words)
  • 2. LinkedIn video (60-90s edit, MSP framing, "werkt ook voor..." tag)
  • 3. Newsletter entry (biweekly digest)
  • 4. Homepage social proof rotator ("recente use cases")

Target cadence: 2 recordings per week = 2 articles + 2 LinkedIn videos. Six months = 50 articles. At current Dutch Claude keyword density, this is decisive volume.
