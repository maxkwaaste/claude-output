Draft Outreach Emails

Three email templates tailored to the segments above. Each follows the anti-pattern rules from the nudge engine: no "circling back," no "touching base," no "I hope this finds you well." Direct, honest, reference-specific.

Template 1: Segment A -- High-Value Check-In

For top Proxuma ARR customers. Goal: open a conversation about what they use, what they skip, and what would make the Projects module more valuable.

To: [Contact Name]
Subject: Quick question about your Projects setup

[First name],

You've been on Proxuma for a while now, and I wanted to ask something specific: how much of the project planning side are you actually using day-to-day?

We've been building out the capacity view and the drag-and-drop scheduling for service calls, and I want to make sure you're getting full value from those. Some of our MSPs run their entire delivery operation through the timeline view. Others stick to the basics.

No pitch here. I'm genuinely trying to understand which parts land and which parts don't. If you have 10 minutes this week, I'd like to hear what works and what doesn't.

Max

Template 2: Segment B -- Dxfferent Crossover

For customers with Dxfferent consulting history. They already know the methodology. Goal: connect the consulting framework to the Proxuma platform features.

To: [Contact Name]
Subject: Bringing the Dxfferent process maps into Proxuma

[First name],

You went through the Dxfferent process with us, and I know [Company] walked away with solid process maps for project delivery. I wanted to check whether you've been translating those into the Proxuma Projects module.

The reason I ask: we built project templates specifically for MSPs who did the Dxfferent assessment. The idea is that your 4-6 most common project types (the ones that make up 75% of revenue) each get their own template with pre-built milestones, resource allocation, and timeline views. That way your engineers never need to leave Autotask, but your project managers get a real planning layer on top.

If you're interested in setting that up, it's a 20-minute session. We configure the templates based on what came out of your assessment.

Worth doing?

Max

Template 3: Segment C -- Discovery Check-In

For active subscribers where we have limited visibility into their usage. Goal: learn how they use the platform, discover untapped features.

To: [Contact Name]
Subject: How is [Company] using Proxuma these days?

[First name],

Honest question: I want to make sure you're getting the value you signed up for.

We've added a lot to the platform since you joined, particularly around project scheduling and the capacity timeline. But I don't have a clear picture of what your team actually uses vs. what's sitting untouched.

Would you be open to a 15-minute call? I'd like to understand your workflow and make sure nothing useful is hiding behind a feature you haven't explored.

No strings. If everything is working fine, that's great too.

Max

Bottom Line

The Proxuma customer base is 3x larger than previously measured (60 vs. 20). The 28 "invisible" customers in Segment C represent the biggest opportunity: they pay, they use the platform, but we have zero visibility into their feature adoption. A simple check-in campaign to these 28 customers will surface the actual usage patterns and identify the best candidates for Projects module deepening and Power BI upsell.

Next Steps

  1. Fill contact gaps: 18 customers have no contact in Forge or HubSpot. Check superadmin user list or Stripe billing emails to find primary contacts.
  2. Send Segment A emails first: 10 high-value customers with contacts -- low volume, high-touch. Personalize each one with specific knowledge from Open Brain.
  3. Resolve Segment D billing: 6 customers with unpaid/canceled status need retention attention before outreach.
  4. Launch Segment C campaign: After A and D, send the discovery check-in to the 28 invisible customers. Track who responds.
  5. Add usage analytics: Consider adding a PostHog dashboard for project module engagement (projects created, timeline views, service call drags) so future outreach is data-driven, not inferred.
