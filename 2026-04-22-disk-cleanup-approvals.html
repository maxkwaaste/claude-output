Chrome profile data
~/Library/Application Support/Google
8.6 GB

What it is: Your entire Chrome profile — saved passwords, cookies, open tabs, history, extensions (including your AdBlock / 1Password / claude-in-chrome extension), autofill, and the browsing cache. The cache is the fat part; everything else is tiny by comparison.

What it affects: Do not rm -rf this folder. Doing so would log you out everywhere, wipe open tabs, break claude-in-chrome, and lose bookmarks not synced to Google. The clean way is Chrome's own "Clear browsing data" dialog — it only removes cached images/files and leaves logins/extensions alone.

After action: Chrome → ⋮ menu → Clear browsing data → Advanced → time range "All time" → tick only "Cached images and files" → Clear. Typically recovers 2-4 GB. First page loads on sites are slightly slower until cache warms back up.

Action: You do this one in Chrome UI; I'll confirm delta afterward
