1. Autotask Status to Kanban Column Mapping

5. Five Demo Scenarios

Each scenario represents a real MSP use case. Set up all five as saved views in the demo environment so you can switch between them during a demo call.

Scenario 1 Dispatch Lead Morning Board
WHO USES THIS: Service desk manager, dispatch coordinator

The story: It's 8:30 AM. The dispatch lead opens Proxuma and needs to know: what's on fire, what's stuck, and who's overloaded. This board answers all three questions in one screen.

Board config:

  • Columns: 5-column default (Backlog > Planned > In Progress > Waiting > Done)
  • Swimlanes: Priority (P1 Critical at top, always expanded. P2 High expanded. P3/P4 collapsed)
  • Card fields: Title, priority badge, customer name, assignee avatar, due date, SLA indicator
  • Filters: Exclude "Complete" older than 7 days. Exclude "Merged Tickets" queue
  • WIP limit: In Progress capped at 30 (shows red counter when exceeded)
  • Entity types: Tickets + Service Calls

Demo moves:

  1. Show P1 swimlane with 5 critical tickets. Point out the overdue badge on one.
  2. Drag a ticket from "Backlog" to "In Progress" -- status updates in Autotask instantly.
  3. Drag across swimlanes (reassign from one tech to another) -- show the field update.
  4. Collapse P3/P4 -- "fold the noise, focus on what burns."
  5. Toggle to Assignee swimlane -- show workload distribution in one click.
Scenario 2 Escalation / L2 Engineering Board
WHO USES THIS: L2/L3 engineers, escalation manager

The story: Escalated tickets need more structure. The L2 team doesn't want to see every new ticket from L1. They want only what's been assigned to their queue, with clear visibility on what's waiting on a vendor vs. waiting on the customer.

Board config:

  • Columns: 6-column (New > Planned > Working > Waiting Client > Waiting Vendor > Done)
  • Swimlanes: Assignee (each engineer sees their lane)
  • Card fields: Title, ticket number, customer, priority, hours worked, SLA countdown
  • Filters: Queue = "L2 Support" only. Active tickets only (exclude Complete > 30 days)
  • WIP limit: 5 per engineer in "Working" column
  • Entity types: Tickets only

Demo moves:

  1. Show the split between "Waiting Client" and "Waiting Vendor" -- "no more guessing who's blocking."
  2. Drag a ticket from "Waiting Client" to "Working" when customer responds -- auto-updates status to "In progress."
  3. Show WIP limit on an engineer who has 5/5 -- "this protects your team from overload."
  4. Point at SLA countdown on a card -- "you see the deadline without opening the ticket."
Scenario 3 Project Implementation Board
WHO USES THIS: Project manager, implementation lead

The story: An MSP is rolling out a new Microsoft 365 tenant for a customer. The project has 20+ tasks, some tickets from issues that came up, and scheduled service calls for onsite work. All three entity types on one board.

Board config:

  • Columns: 5-column (Backlog > Planned > In Progress > Blocked > Done)
  • Swimlanes: Project (group by Autotask project name)
  • Card fields: Title, entity type indicator (color-coded: purple=ticket, blue=task, green=service call), assignee, estimated hours, due date
  • Filters: Show only project-linked items. Include tasks + tickets + service calls
  • WIP limit: None (project work is milestone-driven)
  • Entity types: Tasks + Tickets + Service Calls (all three)

Demo moves:

  1. Show all three entity types with their color headers -- "tasks, tickets, and service calls in one view."
  2. Expand one project swimlane, collapse others -- "focus on the active deployment."
  3. Drag a task from Planned to In Progress -- task status updates in Autotask project.
  4. Point out a service call card with scheduled date -- "your onsite visit is right here on the board."
  5. Killer line: "TopLeft can't do this. Autotask can't do this. Three entity types on one board."
Scenario 4 Account Manager / Customer Review Board
WHO USES THIS: Account manager, customer success

The story: Before a QBR (quarterly business review) call, the account manager wants to see all open work for a specific customer. Or during a customer call, they need a quick view of "what are we working on for you right now."

Board config:

  • Columns: 4-column (To Do > In Progress > Waiting > Done)
  • Swimlanes: Customer (one row per company)
  • Card fields: Title, priority, assignee, last activity date, hours worked
  • Filters: Filter to specific customer(s) for the meeting. Queue = Customer Success or All.
  • Entity types: Tickets + Tasks

Demo moves:

  1. Filter to one customer -- "this is your QBR prep screen."
  2. Show the count badges -- "8 open items, 3 waiting on the customer."
  3. Save this as a named view -- "My QBR: Byte IT" -- click save.
  4. Switch to a different saved view for another customer -- instant context switch.
  5. Point: "This replaces the spreadsheet your AM maintains manually."
Scenario 5 Field Service / Onsite Board
WHO USES THIS: Field service coordinator, onsite engineers

The story: The MSP has a team that goes onsite. They need to see scheduled service calls, onsite tickets, and hardware-waiting items. The "Waiting on material" status is critical here -- hardware orders block onsite visits.

Board config:

  • Columns: 5-column (Scheduled > Dispatched > Onsite > Waiting Parts > Completed)
  • Swimlanes: Assignee (field engineer)
  • Card fields: Title, customer, scheduled date/time, location, priority, contact name
  • Filters: Queue = "Onsite Support." Entity = Service Calls + Tickets with onsite flag.
  • Column mapping: Planned > Scheduled, In Progress > Onsite, Waiting on material > Waiting Parts
  • Entity types: Service Calls + Tickets

Demo moves:

  1. Show service call cards with scheduled time -- "your field team sees their day at a glance."
  2. Drag from "Scheduled" to "Onsite" -- engineer has arrived, status updates.
  3. Move to "Waiting Parts" -- "hardware hasn't arrived, visit postponed. The dispatcher sees it immediately."
  4. Show the assignee swimlane -- "who's where today."

7. Drag-and-Drop Status Rules

When a card is dragged between columns, the Autotask status needs to update. Not all moves are valid. The kanban should enforce Autotask's business rules and block impossible transitions.

Column transition matrix

PlannedNew (1)--In progress (8)Waiting Customer (7)Complete (5)

Immutable fields on drag When dragging across swimlanes, some fields can update (assignee, queue, priority) but others are locked by Autotask: project and customer/company cannot change via drag. The board should show a "locked" indicator and block the drop. This is a feature, not a bug: "We protect you from mistakes Autotask would reject anyway."

8. Card Field Zones

Each card has four zones that can be configured per saved view:

ZonePositionDefault FieldsOptions
Header bandTop, color-codedTitle, entity type indicator, status pillTicket number, project name
Top-rightBadge areaPriority badgeSLA indicator, hours worked
BodyMain contentCustomer name, due dateQueue, contract, issue type, description preview
FooterBottom rowAssignee avatar(s), last activityEstimated hours, billable flag, AI scheduling tag

Two density modes: Compact (header + priority only, for high-volume boards) and Comfortable (all four zones visible, for detailed work).
