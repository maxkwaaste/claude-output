Questions that block building

Blocker
1. Recording consent and GDPR
The spec says every Teams call gets auto-recorded with a banner notification. In the Netherlands, that has AVG/GDPR weight. External prospects will see "Recording has started" on every call.

Have you talked to anyone (lawyer, Dxfferent partners) about this? How do you think your prospects will react? If some push back, you'd need a way to turn off recording per call -- which means the pipeline goes from "we get everything" to "we get most things."

2. Two tenants -- who decides for Dxfferent?
Auto-record and auto-transcribe need to be turned on for both Proxuma and Dxfferent. Proxuma is your call. But Dxfferent -- who makes that decision? If it takes weeks to approve on the Dxfferent side, the whole transcript pipeline is stuck.

3. Does Forge actually exist yet?
The spec references Forge CRM everywhere as the place data goes. But the prerequisites table says "Forge CRM data model: needs definition." So honestly -- is Forge a running system you can write data to today? Or is Forge itself something that needs to be built first?

If Forge is still mostly an idea, then Pillars 2, 3, and 4 all wait on it, and the real first step is "build Forge," not "build the transcript pipeline."

4. What shape are the 140+ transcripts in?
You mention 140+ demo transcripts in Open Brain. Are these actual .vtt files with timestamps and speaker labels ("Max said X, prospect said Y")? Or are they more like notes and summaries you captured after calls?

The playbook quality depends entirely on this. Clean transcripts with real dialog means the AI can find real patterns. Summaries mean the playbook is based on your memory of what happened, not what actually happened.
