Tradeoffs at a glance

Dimension A. Navy + Electric B. Cobalt + Cyan C. Indigo + Sky
Personality Corporate, serious, safe. Reads as "enterprise SaaS default." Tech-forward, energetic, optimistic. Signals modern infra + AI. Modern SaaS standard. Matches Linear, Vercel, Stripe territory.
Glow capability Weak. Electric blue doesn't bloom well, reads flat on white. Strong. Cyan is the only hue here that genuinely glows (aurora + screenshot halos). Medium. Sky blue is soft; blooms gently but lacks energy.
Dark mode payoff Electric blue holds up but feels generic on dark. Excellent. Cyan on dark navy is peak "intelligent product" aesthetic (Anthropic, OpenAI territory). Indigo washes out slightly against dark bg. Sky fades.
Logo continuity Breaks from current Proxuma sky blue entirely. Breaks from logo too, but cobalt is close enough to read as "same family." Highest. Keeps Proxuma sky blue as accent — logo stays on-brand.
Differentiation Low. Thousands of B2B SaaS sites use this. High. Few MSP/Autotask tools use cobalt+cyan. Teamwork equivalent in blue space. Low. Indigo is over-used in 2025-2026 SaaS design.
Contrast / accessibility Strong. Both colors pass WCAG AA on white easily. Strong on anchor, cyan needs care on white (use for accents/icons, not body text). Indigo passes AA. Sky blue fails contrast on white — accent only, never text.
AI module framing Generic. Looks like any AI feature. Strongest. Cyan reads as "intelligent / data / signal" — matches AI narrative. Indigo reads as "creative / design." Less aligned with data/AI.

Why B wins for Proxuma specifically

Three things about your context push this toward Cobalt + Cyan:

  • You're building an AI module. Cyan is the signal color of intelligence across the category (Anthropic's branding, OpenAI's gradients, Vercel's Obsidian theme). It pre-sells the AI story visually before the copy does.
  • You want dark mode to come live. Cobalt+cyan is the pairing that looks best when the lights go off. Indigo+sky loses energy in dark; navy+electric looks corporate.
  • You want a Teamwork-equivalent in blue. Teamwork's pink+purple works because purple genuinely glows. Cyan is the only option here with equivalent luminosity for aurora/bloom treatments around product screenshots.
Logo compatibility check Your current Proxuma sky blue (~#7FA8D8) can live on as a tertiary in Option B — use it for section dividers, subtle backgrounds, and tags. The cobalt anchor replaces it for CTAs and emphasis. You don't have to abandon the logo; you're graduating it to a fuller palette.
Bottom line

Cobalt anchor, cyan accent, Proxuma sky as tertiary

Use #1e40af for CTAs and headline emphasis, #06b6d4 for accents, inline highlights, icons, and glow effects. Keep #7FA8D8 as a tertiary for soft backgrounds and brand continuity with the existing logo. This gives you a three-tone blue system that is distinctive in the MSP/Autotask space, glows in dark mode, and frames the AI module without needing new copy to explain it.

If you want to explore further

  • Tighten cobalt to #1d4ed8 if you want a touch more saturation on CTAs.
  • Replace cyan with #22d3ee (lighter) for a more "playful AI" read, or #0891b2 (deeper) for more gravitas.
  • Add a warm neutral (sand #f5efe4 or cream #faf6ee) as a fourth tone to break up all-blue fatigue on long marketing pages.
