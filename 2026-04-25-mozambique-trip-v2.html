Flights & Transfers

Getting In: Munich to Vilanculos

Option A (recommended): Munich → Johannesburg (Lufthansa, ~10h, direct, A350) → Vilanculos (Airlink, ~2h15). Arrive Aug 31 midday if taking early JNB connection.

Option B: Munich → Maputo (via JNB or Addis Ababa) → Vilanculos (LAM, ~1h15, 4x/week). Adds a Maputo overnight if connections don't align.

Est. cost: $700-1,100pp Munich-Vilanculos. Lufthansa MUC-JNB from ~550 EUR, Airlink JNB-VNX from ~$190.

Vilanculos ↔ Bazaruto/Benguerra

Boat transfer: Speedboat 30-45 min ($50-100pp round trip). Most lodges arrange this. Dhow safari option: 2-3 hours, more scenic, ~$40-80pp.

Helicopter: 10-15 min, $200-300pp one-way. Only worth it if the lodge includes it.

Bazaruto/Benguerra to Tofo (the tricky bit)

Key logistics decision There is no direct flight from Vilanculos to Inhambane/Tofo. Three options:
Option Time Cost (2 pax) Verdict
A. Fly VNX-INH 45 min flight ~$150-200pp Best option. LAM flies this 4-5x/week.
B. Drive EN1 highway 5-6 hours ~$100-150 (private transfer) Cheap but eats a full day. Road quality is decent.
C. Fly via Maputo ~5h total (2 flights + layover) ~$400-500pp Expensive and slower than direct VNX-INH.

Recommendation: take the LAM flight VNX-INH on Wednesday Sep 3 (returning from island that morning, flying afternoon). The 45-min hop runs ~$150-200pp and saves the whole day. Then 25-min taxi from Inhambane airport to Tofo.

Monday Sep 7: Tofo to Maputo to Windhoek

LAM Inhambane → Maputo: Flies Mondays, but departure is ~15:30 (afternoon, not morning). Flight time 1h.

Alternative: Drive Tofo → Maputo early morning (~6-7 hours via EN1). Leave at 05:00, arrive ~11:00-12:00. This works better for catching an afternoon/evening Airlink to Windhoek (via JNB).

Airlink Maputo → Windhoek: Via Johannesburg. Maputo-JNB ~1h, JNB-WDH ~2h. Multiple daily connections. Need to confirm Sep 7 schedule.
Monday morning timing The LAM Inhambane flight leaves at 15:30, not morning. If you want to leave Tofo in the morning, driving is the only option. Leave Tofo at 05:00, drive to Maputo (~6h), catch an evening flight to JNB then WDH. Alternatively, shift the Windhoek leg to Tuesday Sep 8 and take the afternoon LAM flight comfortably.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

1
Monday, August 31
Arrive Vilanculos

Arrive Vilanculos via JNB (Airlink). Check in, settle. Late afternoon dhow sunset sail on the bay with views of the Bazaruto Archipelago. Dinner at a beachfront restaurant.

Stay: Vilanculos Beach Lodge or Samara Lodge. Mid-range, beachfront, pool. ~$80-120/night for a double.
2
Tuesday, September 1
Vilanculos

Full day in Vilanculos. Options: snorkeling day trip to Magaruque Island (closest island, 2h by dhow or 20 min speedboat), horse riding on the beach, explore the local fish market, or just hang at the beach.

Diving: Two Mile Reef is accessible as a day trip from Vilanculos. Good coral, reef fish, occasional mantas. Dive operators in town charge ~$60-80 per 2-tank dive.
3
Wednesday, September 2
Bazaruto or Benguerra Island

Morning speedboat transfer from Vilanculos (~30-45 min). Check into island lodge. Afternoon snorkeling or first dive on the house reef. Sundowners on the beach.

September marine life: Humpback whales at peak migration (100+ spotted in a single day). Reef manta rays at cleaning stations. Dugong sightings possible. Coral gardens in the marine national park.
4
Thursday, September 3
Bazaruto or Benguerra Island

Full day on the island. Morning dive (Two Mile Reef or house reef). Afternoon options: walk the inland sand dunes and freshwater lakes on Bazaruto, or kayak/paddleboard on Benguerra. Humpback whale watching from the shore or by boat.

Late afternoon: speedboat back to Vilanculos. Evening: fly VNX-INH (LAM, 45 min) if available, or overnight in Vilanculos and fly next morning.

5
Friday, September 4
Praia do Tofo

Arrive Tofo (25 min taxi from Inhambane airport). Check into beachfront lodge. Afternoon dive with one of Tofo's PADI centers. Tofo is the manta ray capital of Mozambique: year-round resident population at Manta Reef.

Stay: Hotel Tofo Mar ($80-150/night, boutique beachfront, 8 rooms) or Liquid Dive Adventures lodge ($70-100/night, beachfront cabins, on-site dive center). Both mid-range and walking distance to bars/restaurants.
6
Saturday, September 5
Praia do Tofo

Full day diving. Morning 2-tank dive: Manta Reef + The Office (cleaning station). Afternoon: ocean safari for humpback whales (boat trip, ~$40-60pp). Evening: Tofo's bar scene, fresh seafood at Dino's or Casa de Comer.

Tofo dive sites: Manta Reef (reef mantas year-round), The Office (cleaning station), Tofinho (whale sharks Oct-Mar, humpbacks Jun-Oct), Giants Castle, Amazon. Visibility 10-25m in September.
7
Sunday, September 6
Praia do Tofo

Last full day. Morning dive or sleep in. Walk to Tofinho Beach (rocky headland, tide pools). Afternoon: relax, pack. Final sunset dinner on the beach. Early night -- long transfer day tomorrow.

8
Monday, September 7
Tofo → Maputo → Windhoek

Early morning departure from Tofo. Drive to Maputo (~6-7h, depart 05:00). Or: take the 15:30 LAM flight from Inhambane and add an overnight in Maputo/shift Windhoek to Sep 8.

Onward: Airlink Maputo → JNB → Windhoek. Multiple daily connections. Total travel time ~5-6h including layover.

Bazaruto vs. Benguerra Island

Factor Bazaruto Island Benguerra Island
Size Largest island (37km long) Smaller, more intimate (11km)
Landscape Sand dunes, freshwater lakes, forests, flamingos Pristine beaches, tropical vegetation
Diving Good, Two Mile Reef nearby Better house reef right offshore
Budget option Pestana Bazaruto Lodge: ~$465/night all-inclusive andBeyond Benguerra: ~$1,050+/night pp (luxury tier)
Vibe More variety, exploration, nature walks Pure luxury barefoot island, snorkel-off-the-beach
Wildlife Crocodiles in lakes, flamingos, diverse birdlife Marine-focused: turtles, reef life, dolphins
Recommendation Bazaruto Island with Pestana Lodge gives the best balance for your trip. At ~$465/night all-inclusive (vs. $1,050+pp on Benguerra), you save $1,000-2,000 for just 2 nights and still get great diving, humpback whale watching, and the bonus of walking the inland dunes and freshwater lakes. Benguerra is the splurge choice if budget allows -- the house reef is genuinely better for snorkeling straight off the beach.

Pre-Trip Checklist

Documents

  • German passport: ETA required from evisa.gov.mz (visa-exempt but ETA mandatory since Feb 2026)
  • Namibia: visa-free for German passports (90 days)
  • South Africa transit visa: not required for Germans (if connecting in JNB)
  • Travel insurance with diving cover

Book in Advance

  • MUC-JNB-VNX flights (Lufthansa + Airlink)
  • Bazaruto/Benguerra lodge (limited rooms, books out)
  • LAM flights VNX-INH (only 4-5x/week)
  • Dive packages at Tofo (reserve for weekend slots)
  • Airlink MPM-JNB-WDH for Sep 7

September Conditions

  • Weather: Dry season, 22-28C daytime, little rain. Comfortable and sunny.
  • Water: 22-24C, visibility 10-25m. Comfortable in a 3mm wetsuit.
  • Humpback whales: Peak migration. 100+ spotted in a single day around the archipelago.
  • Manta rays: Year-round at Tofo cleaning stations. Reliable sightings.
  • Whale sharks: Building toward peak (Oct-Mar). Possible but not guaranteed in early September.

Open Decisions

  • Bazaruto or Benguerra? Bazaruto (Pestana) saves $1,200+ and has landscape variety. Benguerra (andBeyond) is the barefoot luxury splurge with better house reef.
  • Sep 7 timing: The Monday LAM flight from Inhambane leaves at 15:30, not morning. Either drive Tofo-Maputo at 05:00 (6-7h) or push Windhoek to Sep 8 and fly comfortably.
  • VNX-INH flight day: Check LAM schedule for Thu Sep 3 availability (boat back from island morning, fly afternoon). Backup: drive 5-6h.
  • Cape Town alternative: Still on the table as a stopover instead of Mozambique. Different trip character entirely. Research from Apr 5 saved in Open Brain.
