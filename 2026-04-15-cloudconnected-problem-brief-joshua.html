Can a freelance engineer work in Proxuma without an Autotask license?

CloudConnected has occasional project-only freelancers — sometimes one month a quarter, sometimes less — and refuses to pay for a full Autotask seat for them. Anastasiya called this their "core question." She said solving it would be a game changer. I told her I'd bring it back to you.

Prospect
CloudConnected (NL MSP)
Asked by
Anastasiya, PM
Status
Open — needs answer

01The situation

CloudConnected runs a mixed bench: in-house engineers, full-time remote engineers, and a freelance pool they pull in for project bursts. The freelancers are not uniform.

Engineer type Touches help desk? Autotask license today What they want in Proxuma
In-house help desk engineers Yes, daily Full Stays the same
Freelance help desk engineer Yes Full (already paid for) Stays the same — fine
Project-only freelancer No, never None — too expensive Work on assigned project tasks, log time, get billed — without an Autotask seat

Different freelancers also get sold at different rates. Jarka was clear that a single shared "external" account isn't acceptable: "you would need different resources, because then the rate would be different." Each freelancer needs their own identity in Proxuma even if they have no Autotask seat.

"We have one engineer who works on projects, and he works, let's say, one month a quarter — so it won't make sense to give him a full Autotask license. But we will have to pay the license for him." — Anastasiya, the framing of the problem
"This is our core question. Can we somehow not pay for Autotask licenses for our externals?" — Anastasiya, end of the call

02Why the obvious workarounds don't fit

  1. Buy them all an Autotask license anyway. This is what the customer is explicitly trying to avoid. For an engineer who works one month a quarter, the seat cost is dead weight.
    Rejected by prospect
  2. Share one external "freelancer" license across people. Anastasiya floated this. Jarka killed it on the call: bill rates differ per freelancer, so they each need their own resource record. Also breaks attribution.
    Rejected by Jarka
  3. Give everyone Autotask access, do all real work in Proxuma. I mentioned some existing customers do this. I also told her it's not the most organized option. Doesn't solve the cost question; just hides it.
    Last resort only
  4. Proxuma handles project-only freelancers without an Autotask seat. This is what Anastasiya wants and what I implied is possible. Service calls land in Outlook, freelancer clicks the link, opens the task in Proxuma, runs the stopwatch, logs time. PM-side time entries on their behalf are already supported. The open question is the technicality around tickets.
    Target answer

03What Jarka surfaced — old Autotask subcontractor portal

At the end of the call Jarka brought up an old Autotask feature: a subcontractor portal where externals accessed their assigned work through a portal instead of a full license. She said it "wasn't very good" but it existed for exactly this purpose. Worth knowing whether it's still alive in Autotask, deprecated, or replaced — your answer to her will land harder if you can speak to that history.

04Specifically what I need from you

The question

Can a project-only freelancer work in Proxuma — receive assigned tasks, log time against them, generate billable entries — without consuming an Autotask license? If yes, how does it work technically (resource record, time-entry attribution, billing flow)? If partial, what's lost (e.g. tickets vs project tasks)? If no, what's the cleanest alternative we can offer?

Bonus, if you can: a one-line read on the old Autotask subcontractor portal so I can answer Jarka cleanly.

05Stakes

Anastasiya called this "the core question" and said solving it would be a "game changer." Translation: this is the deal-shaping question, not a feature wish. If we can answer yes, we shorten the eval. If we say no, the freelancer cost calculation gets added to their TCO model and we lose room.

Bottom line

I promised Anastasiya I'd come back with a real answer. Hand me one — yes / partial / no — plus a sentence on the technical mechanism. I'll take it from there.
