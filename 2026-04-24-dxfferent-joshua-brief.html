Andy's SOW: Phase-by-Phase Analysis

Andy wrote his own SOW titled "Autotask Operational Excellence & Integration Audit." This is not a generic wish list -- it's unusually detailed and shows someone who has done this at other MSPs before. He even has a section called "Technical Implementation Advice for the Vendor" spelling out the exact automations he wants. The goal as stated: move Handled IT Partners from a "reactive" ticketing system to a "proactive" service engine.

Assessment: Andy knows what he wants This SOW is more detailed than what most MSPs produce. Andy has clearly run operational transformations before (Tech Data, prior MSP experience). He will hold us to specifics. The upside: less discovery needed, less ambiguity. The risk: if we can't match this level of precision in our roadmap, we lose credibility against competitors who can.

Phase 1: Foundation & SLA Remediation

  • Audit and rebuild SLA sets (Response, Resolution Plan, Resolution targets)
  • Align ticket statuses with SLA clocks ("Waiting Customer" pauses, "In Progress" runs)
  • Verify business hours and holidays so SLA clocks only run during contracted support hours
Standard config Sprint 1

Phase 2: Process Enforcement & Workflow Automation

  • Mandatory categorization: workflow rules to block ticket closure without Issue Type, Sub-Issue Type, Work Type
  • Checklist libraries for Onboarding, Offboarding, Desktop Setup
  • Stale ticket notifications (no activity >24h)
  • "Perfect Ticket" template: ticket categories hiding irrelevant fields, highlighting critical ones (Cruhu scores, IT Glue links)
Workflow rules Sprint 1-2

Phase 3: Integration Audit & Optimization

  • QuickBooks: Work Types to Service Items mapping, "Ready to Bill" flow
  • Datto RMM: Alerts to actionable tickets with correct priorities
  • Cruhu: Writeback to Autotask UDFs for dashboard reporting
  • IT Glue: Side-panel/extension for one-click documentation
  • Nextiva/TimeZest/ScalePad: API user restrictions, data population (warranty dates from ScalePad)
  • Lifecycle Manager X: Integration for "Project Conversion" from aging assets
Heaviest phase Sprint 2-4

Phase 4: Utilization & Time Entry Accountability

  • "Time Entry Lag" report to catch bundling (all 8h entered at end of shift). Target: entry within 30 min of work
  • Overlapping time detection ("Ghost Billing") for simultaneous entries
  • Stopwatch enforcement over manual rounding
  • Utilization targets at 75%, configured against true 40h week minus holidays/PTO
  • Standardize internal task codes (Admin, Training, Travel, Research)
  • Enforcement automations Andy specifically asked for:
  • "Forgot to Stop" alert: ping if stopwatch running >4h
  • Daily time summary email to Michael at 5:30 PM for techs under 6.5h
  • "Incomplete" block: reject time entries with summary <20 characters
Culture change Sprint 3-4

Phase 5a: KPI & Dashboards

Three dashboard sets required:

  • Technician "My Work": Daily time entry, personal SLA performance, real-time utilization. KPIs: tickets closed/day, calls received, talk time, daily utilization%, time entry lag, response time, resolution time, SLA adherence, aging tickets, high-time tickets, stale tickets, re-opened tickets, first call resolution
  • Service Manager (Michael) "Process Compliance": SLA breaches, uncategorized tickets, resource utilization. KPIs: CSAT, tickets closed/day, calls/day, avg talk time, response/resolution time, SLA adherence, aging/stale/re-opened tickets, FCR
  • Executive (Chris & Ralf): Contract profitability, overall CSAT. KPIs: profitability, CSAT, calls/day, avg talk time, devices/users with high tickets, tickets by type/sub-type, tickets by priority
High visibility Sprint 4-5

Phase 5b: Technical Debt & Cleanup

  • Identify and merge duplicate companies/contacts from ConnectWise migration
  • Security audit: all API user permissions reviewed, "Least Privilege" applied
Can run parallel Sprint 1 or 5

Joshua's call: 3 months aggressive, 4 months realistic 3 months (6 sprints) is achievable if Andy truly delivers 10-15h/week and the owners make decisions fast. The bottleneck won't be config -- it'll be (1) getting their service offering / contract matrix documented for QuickBooks mapping, (2) change management when time entry enforcement kicks in (sprint 4), and (3) whether they want native Autotask dashboards or Power BI for the executive view.

4 months (8 sprints) is safer and allows for the inevitable "Andy got pulled into a client emergency for two weeks" scenario. It also gives breathing room for the integrations that depend on third-party support (ScalePad, Nextiva, Lifecycle Manager X).
