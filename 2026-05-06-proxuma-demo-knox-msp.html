2. Company Profile

Size and Growth
Small MSP, roughly 5 people (Mike + Jerry + Matthew + Kyle + Emmanuel). Significant growth in the last 5-6 years, currently accelerating. Mike: "We are really trying to leverage the project management function." They do new customer onboards, Azure migrations, Google-to-Microsoft migrations, endpoint protection rollouts.
Tools in Use
Autotask -- since 2012. Primary PSA. Project management module acknowledged as inadequate.
IT Glue -- confirmed in use.
RMM -- Datto stack implied (Autotask + IT Glue).
Microsoft Planner -- Jerry was experimenting with it as a Kanban alternative.
Power BI -- Mike started playing with it but never got far. Said "Now you're speaking my language" when Max showed Power BI dashboards.
True Methods -- active participant, using roles-to-revenue framework.
AI -- not yet using AI for any operational work.
Pain Points
  • No visibility into resource capacity. Mike cannot answer: "Can I start a new onboard next week or is the team overloaded?"
  • No process for transitioning work from managed services (reactive) to professional services (projects). Ticket escalation to project is ad hoc.
  • Jerry has no good way to organize his work into "waiting on something" vs "actively workable" buckets.
  • Processes are inconsistent. They try a process, it kind of works, then the next customer is different and it breaks.
  • No formal process maps. Mike admitted: "We clearly do not have those processes mapped out."
  • Not using Autotask service calls at all.
  • Project templates exist for onboarding but need improvement. Other project types have no templates.
  • Jerry and Matthew wear both hats (project manager + engineer) due to team size.
Pricing Model
All-in per-seat pricing for their customers. They do not send itemized hourly invoices, but still need to track hours per customer to understand effective hourly rate and profitability.

3. Demo Topics Covered

  1. Dxfferent consulting overview -- Process maps (Lucid Charts), MSP best practices, two-week sprint model, unlimited Teams DM support, service offering review, IT Glue configuration.
  2. Process maps library -- Showed the full library of MSP process maps (ticket processing, planning, project management, onboarding, offboarding, risk management, time entry, etc.).
  3. Capacity view / resource calendar -- Color-coded capacity per person per day. Explained how service calls enable granular planning. Showed overbooking visibility.
  4. Service calls concept -- Explained service calls as the granular planning unit. Drag-and-drop from bucket to calendar. Bidirectional sync with Autotask.
  5. Reserve capacity view (upcoming feature) -- Estimated hours from tasks smeared across calendar days. Could not fully demo it (too new in dev environment).
  6. Kanban board (upcoming feature) -- Mentioned as coming within a month.
  7. Project Gantt view -- Projects as purple bars, phases, tasks, parent-child relationships. Filtering by person, customer, account manager. Quick search for single project view.
  8. Bulk edit operations -- Move all service calls by X days, reassign to someone else, change to Teams meeting, status changes in bulk.
  9. Outlook integration -- Silent calendar updates (no email spam), hyperlinks to Autotask tickets, Teams meeting creation, customer location fetching.
  10. Inline editing -- Edit tasks, copy phases, billing UDFs, custom columns.
  11. Power BI dashboards -- Hours per person per project, capacity view in Power BI, revenue and costs. Mentioned $300/month pricing. Showed AI/MCP integration for natural language queries against Power BI data.
  12. AI planner (future) -- Briefly mentioned automatic scheduling based on priority. Max noted it is still risky due to hallucination concerns.

6. Objections and Concerns

No pricing objections were raised. No objections about switching costs. The main concerns were about readiness, not about the product:

  • "Chicken and egg" -- They don't have processes mapped, but need the tool. Need the tool, but should processes come first? Mike used this phrase explicitly. (~15:42)
  • Cart before the horse -- Mike asked whether they should do consulting first before adopting Proxuma, or both simultaneously. (~1:05:23)
  • No auto-cascade for project delays -- Jerry asked about automatic rescheduling when predecessor tasks slip. Currently manual in Proxuma (drag and drop). AI planner mentioned as future.
  • Feature not demo-ready -- Reserve capacity view was too new to demo properly. Max could not log into the dev environment smoothly.

9. Sales Intelligence

Budget Signals
No pushback on pricing at any point. When Max mentioned $300/month for Power BI dashboards, Mike showed enthusiasm, not hesitation. Mike asked about ROI and maximizing investment, not about whether they could afford it. "Relatively easy barrier to entry, I like that." The all-in seat pricing model for their own customers suggests they think in recurring revenue terms and understand SaaS value.
Decision Process
Mike is the clear decision maker but actively solicits input from Jerry and Matthew. He asked them directly: "You guys disagree or see anything else there?" Kyle (not on call) has influence on the service desk side. The decision is collaborative but Mike drives it. No mention of a board, partners, or other stakeholders.
Timeline
Warm but not urgent. Max suggested they could wait ~1 month for the Kanban + reserve capacity features to ship, and Mike was comfortable with that. The Joshua/Dxfferent session is Tuesday (next week). Expect 2-4 week decision cycle from first demo to trial start.
Competitors / Alternatives
Microsoft Planner -- Jerry was experimenting with Kanban boards there. Not a serious competitor but shows the need.
ConnectWise Manage -- Jerry has prior experience and positively referenced its ticket-list-to-calendar drag-and-drop. He was pleased Proxuma does the same thing.
No other planning or project management tools were mentioned as being evaluated.
Internal Politics
Jerry and Matthew both do project management AND engineering work. They want to be pure project managers but the team is too small. Mike acknowledged this tension: "They would both love to have two or three people underneath them. We're just not there yet." This is a growth-stage MSP where process discipline is the bottleneck to scaling.
True Methods Connection
They are active True Methods participants using the roles-to-revenue framework. This is good for Dxfferent positioning ("applied True Methods"). The referral came from a True Methods pod meeting, which means other pod members may also be prospects.
Upsell Opportunities
Dxfferent consulting (high probability) -- Mike explicitly asked about it and said "I don't want to rebuild the wheel." Matthew proposed the dual-track approach. They need process work badly.
Power BI dashboards ($300/mo) -- Mike showed genuine excitement. Not first priority but a natural phase-two add-on.
IT Glue configuration -- They have IT Glue but Max hinted it needs proper configuration. Another Dxfferent module.
Additional Proxuma licenses -- Starting with 3 users (Mike, Jerry, Matthew). Kyle and Emmanuel are natural expansions once value is proven.

Bottom Line

This is a high-probability deal for both Proxuma and Dxfferent. The prospect has real pain (no capacity visibility, no process maps, Autotask underutilized), budget willingness, and an owner who is self-aware about what he does not know. The dual-track approach (Proxuma + Dxfferent) was the prospect's own idea, not a Max push. Key risk: they are a small team and may struggle to find time to implement changes while doing client work. The Joshua session on Tuesday is the critical next step. Prepare Joshua with this recording and brief him on the emphasis on project management and resource capacity over reactive ticket management.
