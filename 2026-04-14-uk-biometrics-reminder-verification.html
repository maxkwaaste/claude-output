Why this is strong evidence UKVI does not start preparing an application before biometrics are enrolled. The 9 Feb VFS notification ("application sent to UKVI for consideration") and the 16 Feb Decision Making Centre receipt only happen after biometrics are captured. The 8 Apr reminder also says: "If you have already booked or attended an appointment, please ignore this reminder."

What forums say about this exact reminder

Multiple forum threads confirm UKVI sends these reminder emails generically, without checking whether biometrics were already enrolled.

  • Expat Forum: A user contacted UKVCAS via Twitter DM and was told "everything is OK with application, no need to book appointment and email can be ignored." Another caller was told the closure of service points "had overwhelmed the system, and so these emails were sent out to loads of people who used the app without proper screening."
  • JustAnswer (immigration law): Same pattern reported: "the email is very likely issued in error. This often happens."

Why I am not 100% confident

  • Gmail does not contain a specific "biometrics captured" email from VFS — that email is not always sent. The evidence is circumstantial (doc upload + UKVI handover + Decision Making receipt all happened within days).
  • It is theoretically possible a second/duplicate UKVI application was triggered (e.g. through the EU Settlement Scheme path on 1 Apr) and that the reminder relates to a different application. UKVI uses different reference formats (GWF vs UAN) and the 8 Apr reminder does not include any reference number.
  • EU Family Permit processing officially takes up to 24 weeks; you are at week 9. Receiving a reminder while still inside the standard processing window is unusual unless something flagged.
