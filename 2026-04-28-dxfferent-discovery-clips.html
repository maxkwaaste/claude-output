Key Clips

1. The Situation: Why Andy Was Brought In 0:00 - 3:42
Andy is not the owner. He's been brought in by a friend (the owner) to do an overall assessment. The owner is at ~$3M but has no confidence in delivery or client onboarding.
Andy: "He's not confident in the delivery. He's not confident in the ability to onboard clients. He knows that he doesn't have the right skills here. So he brought me on to do an overall assessment, figure out how to streamline operations."
Andy: "We don't have KPIs built out in the system. We don't have SLAs set properly. We don't have any useful dashboards. There's almost no automations built out to help the team."
Action: Textbook Level 2 intake. Everything on Andy's SOW maps to our standard sprint track.
2. Monitor Noise and RMM Chaos 4:07 - 5:08
Only one person looks at the monitors board, and he says "there's a lot of noise." Andy opened a ticket with Kaseya to audit alerts. Max offered Proxuma templates.
Andy: "Our lead supervisor is the only one who looks at that board periodically. And his answer to me is, there's a lot of noise on there."
Action: Quick win. RMM alert audit + our monitoring templates. Could be Sprint 1 deliverable.
3. No AI Adoption by the Team 6:17 - 8:04
Andy uses AI daily (1-2 hours for policy writing, research). His team is not using it at all. Senior tech calls himself a "Google genius" and has never tried Gemini, even though they pay for it.
Andy: "I asked one of our senior technicians... he said that he's a Google genius or something. I said, have you ever thought about using Gemini? He's like, no. We need a lot of help."
Action: AI adoption opportunity. Andy is receptive, team needs training. Cooper AI / Copilot already available but unused.
4. Process Charts and Ticket Triage 9:23 - 11:15
No flow charts. All tickets come in as the same type. No triage, no categorization, no escalation paths. Max explained how IT Glue sidebar shows engineers the steps per ticket type.
Andy: "All tickets in ours right now come in the same. Everything is a service ticket."
Max: "That triage needs to happen. That kicks off a categorization, which then determines the flow that the ticket goes through."
Action: Full ticket lifecycle setup needed. Our process chart library + IT Glue sidebar integration. Core sprint work.
5. Pricing and Service Model Explained 11:59 - 16:26
Max explained the two-week sprint model, $2,500/month Guide-as-a-Service, potential $1-3K startup fee, biweekly 2hr meetings + unlimited Teams DM access. Also mentioned Buddy-as-a-Service as upgrade.
Max: "The first figure I'll give you is 2,500 a month. That's how much Guide-as-a-Service costs."
Andy: "I would much rather us learn how to do some of this stuff ourselves. I want somebody to help me get it done faster and the right way."
Action: Andy wants Guide model (not Buddy). Wants to understand the why. Both Andy + owner will attend biweekly calls.
6. Invoicing Pain Point 19:04 - 20:10
QuickBooks not integrated with Autotask. Manual invoice adjustments every month. The most expensive person in the org is doing this work.
Andy: "Invoicing is a problem right now. They manually adjust invoices every several months and that's got to end."
Action: QuickBooks integration sprint. Needs service offering review first (simplification). Promise: no more incorrect invoices.
7. Power BI and AI Capabilities Demo 26:47 - 29:18
Max demoed the Power BI MCP concept: Autotask + Datto RMM + Microsoft data in Power BI, queried by AI. Reports, QBRs, license audits, security checks. Explained the anti-hallucination approach (embedded DAX queries for verification).
Max: "Your AI is going to query the Power BI and tell you what are my top 10 customers costing me the most time. Generate a QBR report for this customer."
Action: Power BI upsell opportunity ($300/mo). Andy asked about cost. Not immediate priority but strong interest.
8. Competitive Situation and Timeline Pressure 31:52 - 33:34
Andy is talking to at least 2 other vendors. He's not the decision maker. Wants a roadmap showing month-by-month deliverables. Price is "a little more than expected" but sees value.
Andy: "It's a little bit more than I thought. I'm not the decision maker, so I'd have to take that up. I am talking to a couple other people."
Andy: "If you give me kind of a roadmap... what do we think we're going to see benefits in the first month or two or three?"
Action: We need a month-by-month roadmap in the proposal. Specific deliverables per sprint will close this deal.
9. Results Needed in 6-8 Weeks 39:01 - 40:35
Andy needs standardized processes, KPIs enforced and measured, and accurate dashboards within 6-8 weeks. Already built a morning huddle dashboard, starting next Monday. But the data going in is unreliable because there's no enforcement.
Andy: "Within the next 6 to 8 weeks, I need results with standardized processes, KPIs being enforced and measured."
Andy: "In order to make sure the dashboards are accurate, I need to configure the system with enforcement activities to force the team to put the data in."
Action: First 3 sprints (6 weeks) must deliver: ticket workflows with required fields, time entry enforcement, KPI dashboards. Non-negotiable timeline.
10. AI-Connected Autotask (Acceleration Option) 40:35 - 42:21
Max floated connecting Claude directly to their Autotask for rapid configuration. Andy is interested but needs to think about data handling. His stated vision: become a data-driven organization.
Andy: "I want to be a data-driven organization. And I want AI and automation throughout our company because it will just make us better and our clients happier."
Action: Don't push AI access in the proposal. Mention it as acceleration option after trust is established.
