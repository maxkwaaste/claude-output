Key Clips

1. The Situation: Why Andy Was Brought In
00:00 - 03:42
Andy is not the owner. He's been brought in by a friend (the owner) to do an overall assessment and get the business to a scalable model. The owner is at ~$3M but doesn't have confidence in delivery or client onboarding.
Andy: "He's not confident in the delivery. He's not confident in the ability to onboard clients. He knows that he doesn't have the right skills here. So he brought me on to do an overall assessment, figure out how to streamline operations and how to get this business to a scalable model."
Andy: "We don't have KPIs built out in the system. We don't have SLAs set properly. We don't have any useful dashboards. There's almost no automations built out to help the team. They're really operating at a very basic level."
Joshua: This is textbook Level 2 intake. Everything on Andy's SOW maps to our standard sprint track.
2. Monitor Noise and RMM Chaos
04:07 - 05:08
The monitors board is a mess. Only one person looks at it, and his answer is "there's a lot of noise." Andy opened a ticket with Kaseya to audit the alerts. Max offered Proxuma templates for this.
Andy: "Our lead supervisor lead guy is the only one who looks at that board periodically. And his answer to me is, there's a lot of noise on there."
Max: "Oh no, we can help with that as well. We got templates for that."
Joshua: Quick win here. RMM alert audit + our monitoring templates. Could be Sprint 1 deliverable.
3. No AI Adoption by the Team
06:17 - 08:04
Andy uses AI daily (1-2 hours for policy writing, research). But his team is not using it at all. One senior tech calls himself a "Google genius" and has never tried Gemini, even though they're paying for it.
Andy: "I asked one of our senior technicians... he said that he's a Google genius or something. I said, have you ever thought about using Gemini? He's like, no, I haven't. We need a lot of help."
Max: "I'm not kidding, Andy. We're so AI first that we fired half our developers and everyone has a $200 a month subscription."
Joshua: AI adoption opportunity. Andy is receptive, team needs training. Cooper AI / Copilot already available but unused.
4. Process Charts and Ticket Triage
09:23 - 11:15
No flow charts exist. All tickets come in as the same type ("everything is a service ticket"). No triage, no categorization, no escalation paths. Max explained how categorization drives process, and how IT Glue sidebar shows engineers the steps.
Andy: "All tickets in ours right now come in the same. Everything is a service ticket."
Max: "That triage needs to happen. That kicks off a categorization, which then determines the flow that the ticket goes through. And all of that is spelled out in the sidebar that you get in Autotask when you've integrated IT Glue."
Joshua: Full ticket lifecycle setup needed. Our process chart library + IT Glue sidebar integration. Core sprint work.
5. Pricing and Service Model Explained
11:59 - 16:26
Max explained the two-week sprint model, $2,500/month Guide-as-a-Service, potential $1-3K startup fee, and how the monthly fee covers biweekly 2hr meetings + unlimited Teams DM access to the consultant. Also mentioned Buddy-as-a-Service as upgrade option.
Max: "The first figure I'll give you is 2,500 a month. That's how much Guide-as-a-Service costs. That's everything I just described."
Andy: "No, I would much rather us learn how to do some of this stuff and do some of it ourselves. I want somebody else to help me get it done faster and the right way."
Joshua: Andy wants Guide model (not Buddy). Wants to understand the why. Both Andy + owner will attend biweekly calls.
6. Invoicing Pain Point
19:04 - 20:10
QuickBooks is not integrated with Autotask. They manually adjust invoices every several months. The most expensive person in the org is doing this manual labor.
Andy: "Invoicing is a problem right now. They manually adjust invoices every several months and that's got to end."
Max: "One of the promises is no more incorrect invoices. But we'll first take a look at your service offering."
Joshua: QuickBooks integration sprint. Needs service offering review first (simplification).
7. Power BI and AI Capabilities Demo
26:47 - 29:18
Max demoed the Power BI MCP concept: Autotask + Datto RMM + Microsoft data piped into Power BI, then queried by AI for reports, QBRs, security audits, license optimization. Explained the anti-hallucination approach (DAX queries embedded in reports for verification).
Max: "Your AI is going to query the Power BI and tell you what are my top 10 customers costing me the most time. Generate a QBR report for this customer, telling him which hardware devices to upgrade."
Max: "Our custom Power BI skill gives you those reports and then puts the Power BI query in them. So that you can run that in your Power BI environment and see if it's hallucinating."
Joshua: Power BI upsell opportunity ($300/mo). Andy asked about cost. Not immediate priority but strong interest.
8. Competitive Situation and Timeline Pressure
31:52 - 33:34
Andy is talking to at least 2 other vendors. He's not the decision maker (owner is). He wants a roadmap showing what gets done in month 1, 2, 3. Price is "a little more than expected" but sees value in the hours.
Andy: "It's a little bit more than I thought it would be. I'm not the decision maker, so I'd have to take that up. I am talking to a couple other people."
Andy: "If you give me kind of a roadmap to say, in the first month, we think we could accomplish this... what do we think we're going to see benefits in the first month or two or three?"
Joshua: We need a month-by-month roadmap in the proposal. Andy is comparing us against 2+ vendors. Specific deliverables per sprint will close this.
9. Results Needed in 6-8 Weeks
39:01 - 40:35
Andy needs standardized processes, KPIs enforced and measured, and dashboards within 6-8 weeks. He's already built a basic morning huddle dashboard. Starting it next Monday. But the data going in is unreliable because there's no enforcement.
Andy: "Probably within the next 6 to 8 weeks, I need to have results that we're seeing with standardized processes, KPIs being enforced and measured."
Andy: "In order to make sure the dashboards are accurate, I need to configure the system with enforcement activities and processes to force the team to put the data in."
Joshua: First 3 sprints (6 weeks) must deliver: ticket workflows with required fields, time entry enforcement, KPI dashboards. Non-negotiable timeline.
10. AI-Connected Autotask (Acceleration Option)
40:35 - 42:21
Max floated the idea of connecting Claude directly to their Autotask for rapid configuration. Andy is intrigued but needs to think about data handling. His vision: become a data-driven organization with AI and automation throughout.
Andy: "I want to be a data-driven organization. And I want AI and automation throughout our company because it will just make us better and our clients happier."
Max: "We can get a rapid implementation done. Because we have a framework, a giant spreadsheet for every setting in Autotask."
Joshua: Don't push AI access in proposal. But mention it as acceleration option for after trust is established.
