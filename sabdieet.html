Section 01

Preconception Optimization

The biological blueprint is set in the months before conception. Both parents prepare the terrain.

The Six-Month Rule

Dr. Ghulmiyyah

Both parents commit to at least six months of preparation. Sperm quality matters as much as egg quality. Epigenetic markers carried at conception influence the child's lifelong health.

  • •Spermatogenesis cycle is roughly 74 days, give it two full cycles
  • •Eliminate alcohol, nicotine, recreational drugs
  • •Address dental health, gut health, and chronic inflammation

Metabolic Baselines

Dr. Casey Means

Conception on a metabolically healthy body changes everything. Confirm your baselines with bloodwork before trying.

70-85

Fasting Glucose mg/dL

<100

Triglycerides mg/dL

60-90

HDL mg/dL

Hormonal Prep

Dr. Carrie Lynn Carda

Hormones respond fast to dietary inputs. Strip the diet of inflammatory foods and sodium becomes critical for adrenal and thyroid function.

  • •Eliminate refined sugar entirely
  • •Remove white foods: flour, rice, sugar, processed grains
  • •Switch to Celtic sea salt or Himalayan salt for trace minerals

Iron Management

P.D. Mangan

Iron is a double-edged sword. Excess iron drives the Fenton reaction, generating hydroxyl radicals that damage DNA. Most pregnancy supplements over-dose iron without measuring need first.

40-60

Ideal Ferritin ng/mL

>80

Risk Threshold

Test ferritin and transferrin saturation before supplementing. Donate blood if levels run high.

Section 02

Trimester-by-Trimester Nutrition

An animal-based foundation, free of industrial seed oils, with monitoring throughout.

The Animal-Based Paradigm

Dr. Saladino

The most nutrient-dense human diet centres on ruminant meat, organs, raw dairy, fruit, and honey. These foods deliver the bioavailable forms of every nutrient a developing baby needs.

Grass-Fed Meat

Heme iron, complete protein, B12, zinc, creatine

Organ Meats

0.5 oz fresh liver per day, the original prenatal multivitamin

Raw Dairy

Calcium, K2, A, intact enzymes if tolerated

Fruit & Honey

Clean carbohydrate, polyphenols, ready energy

Eliminate Industrial Seed Oils

Canola, soybean, sunflower, grapeseed, safflower, corn, cottonseed. These oxidise during processing and embed inflammatory linoleic acid into cell membranes.

Cook with: tallow, ghee, butter, coconut oil, extra virgin olive oil (cold use).

Limit Anti-Nutrients

Grains and beans contain oxalates, phytic acid, and lectins that bind minerals like zinc, iron, and calcium, blocking their absorption. Reduce or eliminate during pregnancy.

CGM Monitoring

Dr. Means

A continuous glucose monitor reveals which foods spike your glucose. Physiological insulin resistance increases naturally in the second and third trimester to shunt glucose to the baby, so monitoring matters more, not less.

Aim to keep post-meal glucose under 120 mg/dL. Walk for 10 minutes after every meal to blunt the curve.

Trimester Focus

Trimester Key Nutrients Practical Focus
First Choline, Folate (as methylfolate), B12 (methylcobalamin) Nausea relief: raw dairy, egg yolks, bone broth, ginger, salt
Second Calcium, K2, heme iron, saturated fats More raw dairy, red meat, organ meat. Begin CGM monitoring
Third Marine Omega-3 (EPA & DHA), high protein Blood sugar stability, smaller and more frequent meals

Section 03

Targeted Supplementation

Form matters more than dose. The body responds to bioavailable, methylated nutrients.

Critical Distinction

Folic Acid vs Methylfolate

Between 44 and 60 percent of people carry a MTHFR mutation that prevents conversion of synthetic folic acid into the active form. Worse, unconverted folic acid blocks the folate receptor, starving the developing baby of the active folate it needs.

Avoid

Folic Acid

Synthetic, often added to fortified flour and standard prenatals

Take

Methylfolate (5-MTHF)

Bioavailable form, no conversion required

Brecka's Core Stack

Methylfolate

Active folate, supports neural tube

Methylcobalamin B12

Active B12 for methylation

P-5-P (B6)

Active B6 for nervous system

Magnesium Glycinate

Sleep, muscle, anti-cramp

Vitamin D3 + K2

Bone health, calcium routing

Marine Omega-3 EPA/DHA

Brain and eye development

Iron Caution

Avoid blanket synthetic iron prescriptions like ferrous sulfate. Get heme iron from red meat and liver first. Supplement only if labs confirm deficiency, and even then use a gentler form like iron bisglycinate.

Section 04

Movement & Strength Training

Pregnancy is not an illness. The strong body delivers, recovers, and carries the infant better.

Dr. Ghulmiyyah

No Bed Rest

Movement is medicine. The default prescription should be activity, not rest.

5 days per week, 30 min

Mangan

High-Intensity Strength

Slow sets to failure preserve and build muscle. Posterior chain focus protects the lower back.

2x per week, 30 min sessions

Bryony Deery

Pregnancy Pilates

Breath-led core work. No crunches or sit-ups. Elevated planks, side-lying series, pelvic floor coordination.

3-4x per week

Weekly Framework

Modality Frequency Purpose
HIT Strength 2x per week Maintain muscle, posterior chain, deadlift patterns
Pilates 3-4x per week Pelvic floor, core stability, breath coordination
LISS Walking Daily, post-meal Glucose disposal, circulation, mood

Section 05

Toxin Reduction & Environment

The placenta is selective but not invincible. Reduce the load.

Eliminate Endocrine Disruptors

  • Phthalates (in fragrances, vinyl, plastics)
  • Parabens (in cosmetics, lotions)
  • Synthetic fragrances (perfumes, candles, detergents)
  • Plastics with BPA and BPS (water bottles, food storage)

Water Hierarchy

From best to acceptable

    Reduced molecular hydrogen, anti-inflammatory

  2. 2

    Spring water

    Glass-bottled where possible

  3. 3

    Reverse osmosis with mineral drops added back

Section 06

Sleep, Stress, Sunlight

The free, ancestral inputs that shape circadian rhythm and hormone production.

Morning Sunlight

Within 30 minutes of waking. Sets circadian rhythm, supports cortisol awakening response, and primes melatonin for the night.

Sleep 8-9 Hours

Cool, dark, quiet room. One bad night causes acute insulin resistance. Protect this fiercely.

Grounding

Barefoot on grass, sand, or soil for 20+ minutes daily. Equalises body electrical charge with the earth.

Breathwork

Daily nasal breathing practice. Prevents pregnenolone steal where chronic cortisol robs the precursor needed for progesterone.

Section 07

Postpartum Recovery

The first 30 days set the trajectory for the next 30 years of hormonal health.

Critical Window

The 30-Day Hormonal Reset

In the first 30 days after delivery, hormones recalibrate dramatically. The mother needs deep nourishment, warmth, and rest. Skipping this window contributes to thyroid dysfunction, postpartum depression, and slow recovery.

Nutrient Repletion

  • •Organ meats, especially liver and heart
  • •Collagen and bone broth daily
  • •Raw dairy and saturated fats
  • •Continue methylated B vitamins and Omega-3

Gentle Movement Progression

  1. 1

    Breathwork first

    Reconnect diaphragm and pelvic floor

  2. 2

    Walking

    Short, slow, daily

  3. 3

    Restorative Pilates

    After 6 weeks, never sooner

Reject the Pressure

No bounce-back culture. The body just performed a miracle. Recovery is the work, not aesthetics.

Stay warm, eat warm food, sleep when the baby sleeps, accept help, refuse visitors who drain.

Section 08

Daily Regimen & Checklists

A practical synthesis. Print this and stick it on the fridge.

Daily Rhythm

Morning

Sunlight on the skin, barefoot grounding, 10 minutes of nasal breathing before any screens.

Hydration

Hydrogen or spring water with a pinch of Celtic salt. No tap water.

Meals

Animal-based: meat, organs, raw dairy, SMASH fish, organic fruit and honey. Cooked in tallow, ghee, or butter.

Post-Meal

10-minute walk to disperse glucose. CGM data informs which meals deserve longer walks.

Evening

Magnesium bath with Epsom salts. Magnesium glycinate before bed. Cool, dark room, no blue light after sunset.

Do Not Consume

Non-negotiable list. Each item carries a documented mechanism for harm during pregnancy.

  • Folic Acid

    Blocks the folate receptor in MTHFR carriers

  • Industrial Seed Oils

    Oxidised PUFAs, inflammatory linoleic acid

  • Refined Sugar & White Carbs

    Glucose spikes, gestational diabetes risk

  • Tap Water

    Chlorine, fluoride, microplastics, pharmaceutical residue

  • Synthetic Fragrances

    Phthalates and endocrine disruptors

  • Apex Predator Fish

    Mercury and heavy metal bioaccumulation

Core Supplement Stack

The minimum effective stack. Take with food unless noted.

  • Methylfolate (5-MTHF)

    800-1000 mcg, morning

  • Desiccated Beef Organs

    6 capsules daily, with food

  • Vitamin D3 + K2 (MK-7)

    5000 IU D3 / 100 mcg K2, with fat

  • Magnesium Glycinate

    300-400 mg, evening

  • Marine Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)

    2 g combined, with meal

  • Methylcobalamin B12 + P-5-P

    Active forms, morning
