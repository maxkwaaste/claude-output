Channel Distribution Summary

Channel Breakdown

Direct / Recorded Demo (9): Cubics, Aabyss, inTEC, Itum, Server At Work, Arrows ICT, Complex ICT, Anderson Networks, Gyver, Affirm IT, JUICT, Mario Lehoux. These customers found Proxuma through website, newsletters, or organic search and booked demos or requested recorded demos themselves.

DattoCon Event Leads (5): Holland ICT Groep (Dublin), Pritech (DattoCon 2023), Blue Arc IT (DattoCon 2024), Majestic (DattoCon), Algoma Technology (DattoCon 2024). All met at Kaseya/Datto conferences and followed up afterward.

Dxfferent Channel (8): ConXioN, Lemontree, TwentyFour IT, VMhosts, IT Synergy, Van der Hoff ICT, EAC (partial), Steentjes Groep. Joshua and/or Jasper from Dxfferent were actively involved in the sales process, onboarding, or billing discussions.

BeMSP Partner Channel (2): Maiano Informatique, FMI INFOGERANCE. Both French MSPs introduced through BeMSP account managers (Louise Navarro and Sebastien Roux respectively).

Dual Channel (2): Blue Arc IT (DattoCon + Dxfferent intake), VMhosts (Recorded Demo + Jasper meeting).

Key Observations

The strongest acquisition channels outside HubSpot are DattoCon events and the Dxfferent partner relationship. DattoCon contacts tend to be international (Australia, Canada, US) and convert slowly but with high commitment. Dxfferent channel customers are mostly Dutch/Belgian and benefit from Joshua's hands-on involvement in onboarding and Autotask optimization. The BeMSP channel in France is newer (late 2025) but already produced two customers. Direct/recorded demo leads are the most common path and show the broadest geographic spread.
