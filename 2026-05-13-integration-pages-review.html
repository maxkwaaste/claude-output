INTEGRATION PAGES REVIEW

The pages don't explain the product

Someone Googling "Autotask Power BI" lands on a page that describes a completely different product than what Proxuma actually sells. The integration pages read like a traditional BI SaaS (share API keys, get dashboards), while the actual product is: Proxuma builds the data model, you bring your own AI, and you generate reports through MCP. That disconnect is the core problem.

The big problem: product model mismatch

The /powerbi/insights/ page explains the product correctly: connect tools, Proxuma builds the semantic model, publish to Power BI, AI connects via MCP, reports in minutes. Five clear steps. The visitor understands they're buying a data model + AI bridge.

The integration pages tell a different story. "We pull tickets into a unified Power BI model" and "Connected in minutes" with three steps: create API user, share credentials, data starts flowing. This sounds like BrightGauge or MSPbots. Where's the Power BI license? Where's the AI? Where's the MCP connection?

The visitor's confusion A person Googling "Autotask Power BI" clicks through, reads the page, and thinks: "OK so I share my API keys and Proxuma gives me 25 reports. Simple." Then they hit the pricing page or talk to sales and discover they need Power BI Pro, need to connect their own AI, and the reports are generated on demand through MCP. That's a different product than what the page promised.

Issue by issue

1. "Getting Started" skips the actual product
HIGH IMPACT
Three steps shown: create API user, share credentials, data flows. The real flow has five steps (the insights page gets this right). The integration pages should mirror that: connect tools, we build your model, publish to Power BI, connect AI via MCP, generate reports. Hiding the AI and Power BI from the setup makes the product seem simpler than it is, which creates friction at sales time.
2. "25+ reports powered by Autotask" is misleading
HIGH IMPACT
The section implies 25 pre-built reports ship with the product. In reality these are examples of what the AI can generate through MCP. The framing should be: "Here's what your AI can create in under two minutes." The small intro text says "generated using AI" but the heading and card layout suggest a report library, not AI-generated samples.
3. No mention of "Bring Your Own AI" anywhere
HIGH IMPACT
The insights page has a clear BYOAI section. The integration pages don't mention Claude, ChatGPT, MCP, or AI at all (except a passing "AI analysis" in one sentence). This is the product's main differentiator. A visitor should understand within 10 seconds: your data, your Power BI, your AI, our model in between.
4. Power BI is invisible as a requirement
MEDIUM IMPACT
Someone Googling "Autotask Power BI" probably already has or wants Power BI. But the page never mentions that they need a Power BI Pro/Premium license, that data lands in their workspace, or what the Power BI side looks like. The page title says "Autotask PSA + Power BI" but the body treats Power BI as a background detail, not as the platform the customer already uses.
5. Cross-source section is buried and undersold
MEDIUM IMPACT
"Better with friends" is cute but doesn't convey the actual value: combining Autotask with RMM and M365 is the reason to use Proxuma instead of just connecting Autotask to Power BI directly. These cross-source insights (ghost device detection, true profitability, churn prediction) are the competitive moat. They should be higher on the page, not below the report list.
6. Nine draft pages sitting unpublished
MEDIUM IMPACT
Datto RMM, Microsoft 365, HubSpot, SmileBack, and five others all have complete pages but are still draft+noindex. Meanwhile the index page says "Coming soon" for N-able Cove, Inforcer, HiBob. That's search traffic left on the table. Every MSP Googling "Datto RMM Power BI" or "HubSpot Power BI reports" could be landing on your page.
7. Report counts are stale on the index
LOW IMPACT
The index page shows IT Glue at 1 report, but 6 exist now. HubSpot shows 1, but 29 exist. The memory file has the correct counts. When publishing the draft pages, sync the index report counts too.

What a Google visitor actually needs to see

Someone Googling "Autotask Power BI" is an MSP owner or ops manager who already knows Power BI (or wants it) and wants to get their PSA data into it. They're comparing options: do it themselves with Power Automate, use BrightGauge, use MSPbots, or find something else.

The page should answer five questions within 30 seconds of scrolling:

  1. What does Proxuma do that I can't do myself? You get a pre-built semantic model with 600+ measures that cross-references Autotask with your other tools. Building this yourself would take months.
  2. Do I need Power BI? Yes, Power BI Pro or Premium. We publish to your workspace. You own the data.
  3. What's the AI part? You connect Claude, ChatGPT, or any MCP-compatible AI to our Power BI model. Ask questions in plain English, get reports in minutes.
  4. What kind of reports can I get? Here are 25 examples the AI generated. Each one took under two minutes. Your AI generates the same from your live data.
  5. How do I start? Connect your API, we build your model, you're live in 24 hours.

The current page answers #4 and #5 reasonably well (though #5 is oversimplified). It completely skips #1, #2, and #3.

Bottom line

The integration pages are well-designed and the content (data categories, cross-source cards, report lists) is strong. The problem is framing. They present Proxuma as a dashboard service when it's actually a data model + AI bridge. Fix the messaging and publish the 9 draft pages. That's 10 SEO-ready landing pages for every "[tool name] Power BI" search an MSP might run.
