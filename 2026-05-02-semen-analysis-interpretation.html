Motility Breakdown

Sperm motility is graded A through D. Only A and B matter for fertility, because they swim forward.

A: 16%
B: 11%
C: 12%
D: 61% (not moving)
A = fast, straight swimmers  |  B = slower forward swimmers  |  C = moving but going nowhere  |  D = completely still
Why 61% not moving is less alarming than it sounds The percentage of immotile sperm looks high, but two things matter more: (1) the total number of swimming sperm (46.9 million, which is plenty), and (2) the high viscosity likely trapped some sperm that would otherwise have been swimming during the test. A retest with thinner semen may show a better picture.

What to Do Next

  • Mention the high viscosity to a doctor. It is usually harmless but easy to address (drink more water, maintain regular ejaculation frequency before the test).
  • Retest in 2-3 months. Sperm quality varies from sample to sample. The WHO recommends at least two tests before drawing conclusions. Staying well-hydrated before the next test may resolve the viscosity issue and likely improve the motility score.
  • No urgent steps needed. The total swimming sperm count (46.9 million) is comfortably in the range for natural conception. These results do not suggest a fertility problem on the male side.

Disclaimer

This report interprets lab values against WHO 2021 reference ranges (6th Edition). It is not a medical diagnosis. WHO reference values represent the 5th percentile of men who achieved pregnancy within 12 months. Falling below reference does not equal infertility; it means the value is lower than what most fertile men show. Always consult a doctor for clinical assessment. Fertility depends on both partners.
