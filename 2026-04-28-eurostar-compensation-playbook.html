1. Your Legal Rights

Your rights come from two places: EU Regulation 2021/782 (which replaced the older 1371/2007) and Eurostar's own Customer Charter. The regulation is the floor, and Eurostar's charter is slightly more generous in some areas.

EU Regulation 2021/782 1

  • Cancellation: Full refund of the ticket price for the unused portion, or re-routing at the earliest opportunity 2
  • Accommodation: If you're stranded overnight, the railway must provide or pay for a hotel 1
  • Meals: Free meals and refreshments proportionate to the waiting time 1
  • Re-routing right: If no alternative is offered within 100 minutes of scheduled departure, you can arrange your own transport (bus, coach, rail) and claim the costs back 2

Eurostar Customer Charter 3

  • Refund: Full ticket refund processed within 28 days
  • Hotel: "A taxi or hotel will be made available" for late-night delays when public transport is unavailable
  • Expenses: Will reimburse "reasonable costs for hotels or taxis where receipts are provided"

2. Delay Compensation (Bonus on Top)

If the cancellation means you arrive at your final destination late (because you take a later train), you're also entitled to delay compensation. This stacks on top of expenses. 4

Train cancelled from London, no explanation, station office closed. Spent $196 on a hotel, $97 on breakfast, $1,119 on replacement tickets. Initially Eurostar ignored her. After consumer advocate Christopher Elliott intervened, Eurostar refunded everything: original tickets, replacement tickets, hotel, and meals.

Return journey from Netherlands cancelled. No staff at station, customer service unreachable for 3 days. Claimed £800 for hotel, food, and alternative transport. Eurostar agreed to consider hotel costs and "selected few food costs" only. Alternative transport was flat-out refused. Process dragged on over a month with "lost emails" and "website technical issues."

Rick Steves Forum User (June 2025) Claim rejected
Train delayed over an hour (Paris to London). Multiple compensation claims submitted online. All rejected with "delay is under 60 minutes" and "force majeure" despite the passenger clearly experiencing 60+ minutes of delay. Staff on board said the trip would be eligible. The online system disagreed.

Lesson: Delay is measured by arrival time at destination vs. scheduled arrival, not by departure delay. Document your actual arrival time. If the online form rejects you, email traveller.care@eurostar.com directly.

The key pattern: use the help portal email form, be explicit about wanting cash (not vouchers), and cite consumer rights.

For cancellations specifically: you only get a refund or rebooking, no bonus compensation.

Christmas Cancellation (TripAdvisor Thread) Flight cost refused
Family's Eurostar from Amsterdam cancelled on a strike day. Next available train not until Boxing Day. Bought BA flights for £500. Eurostar refused to pay for flights, offering only "a seat on a different train." EU regulation explicitly covers re-routing by rail, coach, or bus, but not flights.

Lesson: Never book a flight as alternative transport. Eurostar won't reimburse it, and the EU regulation doesn't require them to. Only rail/bus/coach alternatives are covered under the 100-minute re-routing rule.

5. Warnings from Other Passengers

Do not accept vouchers by default

Eurostar's online system pushes e-vouchers first. They're valid for 12 months and are worth slightly more (30% vs 25% for delays). But if you don't plan to ride Eurostar again soon, cash is better. Explicitly request a cash refund. 7

Don't book flights as alternative transport

EU regulation covers re-routing by rail, bus, or coach only. Flight costs will be refused. Multiple travelers learned this the hard way. 8

Expect slow processing

Refunds take up to 28 days. Expense claims can take longer. Multiple passengers reported "lost emails" and delayed responses. Follow up weekly by email. If no response after 4 weeks, file a complaint with the national enforcement body or contact a consumer advocate. 9

Keep your original ticket

The flyer explicitly says "retain all receipts AND your original Eurostar tickets for reimbursement." Don't throw away anything until the money is in your account.

Delay is measured on arrival, not departure

If you end up taking a later train and want delay compensation on top, the delay clock is based on when you actually arrive at London St Pancras vs. your original scheduled arrival. Not when the train left. 10
