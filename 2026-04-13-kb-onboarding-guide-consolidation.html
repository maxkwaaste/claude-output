Onboarding Guide: One Long Article, or Roadmap + Detail?

The Proxuma Onboarding Guide v0.5 (1258 lines, 18 chapters) currently lives as one short stub plus 12 separate detail articles on kb.proxuma.com. Max wants this reconsolidated. Three options below, each with tradeoffs. Pick one so we can push.

Current state on kb.proxuma.com

Our push-to-kb.py run on 31 March pushed 13 articles under the Proxuma page. One of those, getting-started/proxuma-onboarding-guide, holds only the intro and the "Reading Paths by Role" table (roughly 129 words). The other twelve are full detail articles derived from the KB-*.md source files in the playbook extract.

The v0.5 source document is designed as a roadmap. Its own preamble says: "For detailed procedures, screenshots, and technical specifications, follow the KB links throughout." There are 45 → [KB: ...] pointers across the 18 chapters, each intended to link out to one of the detail articles.

The tension "One long article" can mean two very different things. Either (a) the roadmap itself becomes long because it absorbs the detail chapters inline, or (b) the roadmap stays a roadmap and only its own body grows to cover all 18 chapters. The answer changes what happens to the 12 siblings.

Option A · Roadmap + Detail (recommended)

Recommended Replace the stub with the full v0.5 body, keep all 12 detail articles
What we do
Action
Overwrite the body of getting-started/proxuma-onboarding-guide with the full 1258-line v0.5 rendered as HTML. Resolve the 45 → [KB: ...] markers to real /kb/proxuma/<topic>/<slug> URLs that already exist on the site. Leave the 12 detail articles untouched.
What a reader sees
Reader experience
One entry-point article that covers the whole onboarding journey in order. Click any chapter's KB pointer and land on a focused detail article with screenshots and step-by-step procedures. Matches the author's original design intent.

Pro Honours the v0.5 design. No content is lost or duplicated. Cross-links work. Detail articles keep their screenshots where they belong.

Pro Cheapest to execute. We only touch one article body and the link markers.

Con Reader still clicks away for deep detail. Not truly "one article" in the strictest sense.

Option B · True Monolith

Maximalist Inline all detail content, delete the 12 siblings
What we do
Action
For each chapter that links to a detail article, splice the detail article's content into the chapter body. Pull screenshots across from the 12 detail articles and embed them inline. Delete (or unpublish) the 12 siblings. Replace the 45 → [KB: ...] markers with in-page anchors.
What a reader sees
Reader experience
A single very long article. Ctrl-F works across everything. No clicking between articles. Probably 15 to 25 thousand words and 30 plus screenshots in one page load.

Pro Literally matches "one long article". Readable start-to-finish. Good for printing or PDF export.

Con Content overlap. The 12 KB-*.md sources already overlap with the v0.5 chapters, so stitching needs editorial judgement to avoid repeating the same paragraph twice.

Con Other existing KB articles (Menu Bar, Filters, Service Calls, etc., about 50 total) still need to link somewhere. Killing the 12 onboarding siblings breaks any inbound links from the wider KB.

Con Page weight climbs fast. Every screenshot we embed loads on every visit.

Option C · Merge Body, Keep Siblings Linked

Hybrid Inline detail content but keep detail articles as duplicates
What we do
Action
Option B's body, but we leave the 12 detail articles on the site as-is. The onboarding article becomes a self-contained long-read; the detail articles continue to serve direct search hits and inbound links from outside the onboarding flow.

Pro Reader gets a monolith. External linkability preserved.

Con Two canonical copies of the same content. SEO cannibalisation risk. Future edits have to happen in both places, or one gets stale.

Open questions either way
  1. v0.4 or v0.5 as source? v0.5 is newer by about two weeks but we have not confirmed it is the final edit.
  2. Images. v0.5 only references three images itself. The screenshots currently live on the 12 detail articles. For Option B or C we would need to carry those over.
  3. Slug. Overwrite the existing proxuma-onboarding-guide slug under Getting Started, or publish under a new slug and redirect?
  4. Cross-link markers. The 45 → [KB: ...] strings need to be resolved to real URLs regardless of option. Some of them may point at articles that were never pushed (need a short audit pass).
Recommendation

Option A

It matches how v0.5 was written, it is the cheapest to execute, and it keeps the 12 detail articles doing the work they were built for. Option B becomes a fair choice only if the intention is a printable, downloadable single-document onboarding manual. Option C carries real SEO and maintenance cost for a small reader gain.
